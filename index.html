      The Dj's List

      DJ Stellar

      Since moving to Las Vegas six years ago, the personable DJ has made a name for himself as a resident at the most distinguished house nights such as Godskitchen and Paul Oakenfold's Perfecto.

      DJ Mia Moretti

      Classically trained musician Moe strums over genre-jumping tracks as spinster Moretti orchestrates the night. The duo, who improvise a majority of their sets, kick off their Cosmopolitan residency Dec. 30.

      DJ Avicii

      The starry-eyed and humble 22-year-old is first a producer, second a DJ. He's received his first Grammy nomination for his track "Sunshine" with David Guetta, and will bring just that to Las Vegas when he spins at Marquee on Jan. 2

      DJ Jesse Marco

      The New York native got his start at the tables at age 12, worked for the iconic Mark Ronson's record label, and was briefly mentored by the late DJ AM.

      DJ Teenwolf

      Began DJing at 17 and used a fake ID to play Las Vegas spots until he became legal two years ago. The down-to-earth DJ spins at Beauty Bar

      DJ Tatiana

      The Santa Monica, Calif., native has toured all over the world, owns her own record label (Jook Music) and was one of the few female DJs to play Electric Daisy Carnival Las Vegas

      Deadmau5

      The 30-year-old man behind the oversize mouse head, is as much an entertainer as he is a composer and producer.

      Zandi

      A longtime industry champion who quickly moved up from cocktail waitress to bartender, then from being one of the first female VIP hosts

      DJ Derrick Anthony

      Formerly known as Big Dee, the radio personality-turned-club-DJ is most often spotted spinning at XS or Tryst these days, where he's been a resident for three years

      DJs Nervo

      The starry-eyed songwriters-turned-DJ sisters Mim and Liv Nervo discovered their calling after inspiring summers spent vacationing in Ibiza, Spain
